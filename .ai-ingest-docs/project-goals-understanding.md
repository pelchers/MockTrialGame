## Project Identity
- **Name:** MockTrial.app
- **One-liner:** Browser-based multiplayer mock trial simulation platform with human and AI courtroom participants.
- **Repository:** https://github.com/pelchers/MockTrialGame.git

## Core Purpose
MockTrial.app is intended to become a complete courtroom simulation engine that can serve multiple audiences: schools, universities, legal training programs, and independent learners or players. The product combines educational fidelity with game-like interactivity, aiming to make realistic trial procedure teachable, replayable, and collaborative inside a browser.

The platform is not limited to a simple mock trial script runner. It is intended to simulate the procedural flow of a trial from pretrial preparation through verdict and selected post-trial outcomes, while supporting multiplayer participation, AI seat filling, spectator interaction, and structured legal reasoning. The design goal is to make courtroom procedure legible, operable, and testable.

## Target Users
- **Primary:** teachers, professors, mock trial coaches, law-related education programs
- **Secondary:** students, debate/mock trial participants, legal trainees, study groups
- **Tertiary:** individual users interested in courtroom procedure, rhetoric, and legal simulation
- **Observers:** spectators who want to watch, learn, and interact through reconstruction tools

## Key Features & Capabilities
- Hosted browser sessions with lobby-based joining
- Human or AI control for courtroom roles
- Structured trial flow covering pretrial, trial, and selected post-trial phases
- Real-time courtroom communication and private side channels
- Rules engine for objections, evidence handling, and judge rulings
- Jury systems for instructions, deliberation, and verdict capture
- AI side-panel tutor and role assistant
- Spectator reconstruction sandbox for event replay, timelines, and demonstrative scenarios
- Research and planning foundation that compares architecture options before final stack selection
