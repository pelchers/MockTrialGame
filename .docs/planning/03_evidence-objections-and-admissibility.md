# Evidence, Objections, and Admissibility

## Why This Matters
Evidence handling is where courtroom fidelity becomes operational. If evidence and objections are reduced to free-form chat, the platform loses much of its educational value.

## Core Evidence Concepts
- exhibit identity and provenance
- offered vs admitted vs excluded status
- demonstrative vs substantive material
- witness linkage and foundation requirements
- chain-of-custody concepts where relevant

## Core Objection Families to Model First
- relevance
- hearsay
- speculation
- lack of foundation
- leading
- argumentative
- asked and answered
- compound
- assumes facts not in evidence
- improper opinion / lay opinion / expert boundary

## Product Modeling Implications
- Objections should be structured actions with timing windows.
- Rulings should update the evidence state and transcript.
- Teaching mode can explain objection rationale in plain language.
- Competition mode may restrict objection menus to supported formats.

## Research Expansion Priorities
- Minimum viable objection set for broad educational value
- Mapping of objection categories to beginner, intermediate, and advanced modes
- How to represent offers of proof, sidebar concepts, and limiting instructions later
