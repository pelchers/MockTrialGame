# Mock Trial Formats and Case Content

## Why This Layer Matters
Real courtroom procedure is only one part of the product. Educational usage also depends on how mock trials are packaged, taught, and scored.

## Research Areas
- High-school and collegiate mock-trial competition formats
- Classroom teaching formats with teacher-led simplification
- Open-play sandbox scenarios for informal users
- Case packet composition: witness statements, exhibits, stipulations, instructions, verdict sheets

## Product Implications
- The platform should support more than one session mode.
- Case packets should be structured and machine-readable where possible.
- Competition overlays may restrict objections, timing, and witness knowledge.
- Teacher-created case content is likely an important later capability.

## Recommended Content Layers
- Canonical scenario metadata
- Role briefs
- Witness knowledge boundaries
- Exhibits and admissibility metadata
- Jury instructions / decision sheets
- Teacher notes and learning objectives
