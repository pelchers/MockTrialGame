# Case Authoring and Content Operations

## Why This Is Critical
The product cannot launch credibly on engine quality alone. It needs real case content, structured editorial workflows, and a repeatable way to publish case packets that work for hosts, players, jurors, and AI systems.

## Launch Content Requirements
- a small canonical case library
- structured witness briefs
- exhibit metadata with admissibility and provenance fields
- stipulations and timeline facts
- jury instructions and verdict sheets
- host notes and facilitation guidance

## Operational Requirements
- versioned case packets
- review and approval workflow
- change tracking when a case is revised
- publish states such as draft, internal review, ready, launched
- content QA for legal accuracy, educational clarity, and gameplay usability

## Schema Direction
Each case should eventually include:
- scenario metadata
- role assignments
- witness knowledge boundaries
- exhibit list and evidence-state metadata
- timeline facts
- instruction and verdict artifacts
- AI-safe structured summaries for bounded role simulation

## Immediate Decision Docket
1. How many canonical launch cases are required?
2. Who authors and reviews them?
3. What is the minimum schema for a launch-ready case packet?
4. Which content fields are required for AI role support?
5. What publishing workflow is needed before implementation starts?
