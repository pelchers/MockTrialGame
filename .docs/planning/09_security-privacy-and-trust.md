# Security, Privacy, and Trust

## Security Goals
- Protect classroom and institutional data
- Preserve authoritative session integrity
- Prevent role leakage between private channels
- Keep AI outputs bounded and auditable

## Privacy Considerations
- Minimize personally identifiable information
- Support school-safe defaults
- Separate public spectator data from private participant data
- Define retention rules for transcripts, recordings, and AI logs

## Trust and Safety Concerns
- Harassment or disruptive roleplay in multiplayer sessions
- Improper AI behavior in legal or educational contexts
- Overconfidence that could be mistaken for legal advice
- Abuse of spectator tools or private communications

## Control Areas
- role-based access control
- audit logging for key actions
- content moderation for messages and uploaded materials
- organization-level policy settings
- AI response labeling and disclaimers

## Planning Recommendation
Treat safety as a first-order architectural concern, not a later moderation add-on. The authoritative event log, role visibility rules, and AI boundary design are part of the trust model.
